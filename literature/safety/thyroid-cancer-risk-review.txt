TITLE: Thyroid Cancer Risk with GLP-1 Receptor Agonists — Safety Review
DRUG CLASS: GLP-1 receptor agonists

BACKGROUND:
GLP-1 receptors are expressed on thyroid C-cells. In rodent studies, GLP-1 RAs caused 
dose-dependent C-cell hyperplasia and medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC). This preclinical 
finding prompted a black box warning and dedicated human safety surveillance.

PRECLINICAL DATA:
- Rats and mice: dose-dependent C-cell hyperplasia and MTC with all GLP-1 RAs
- Monkeys: no C-cell changes observed at clinically relevant exposures
- Species difference attributed to much higher GLP-1 receptor density on rodent C-cells
- Human C-cells have very low GLP-1 receptor expression — mechanistic basis for concern is weak

HUMAN EVIDENCE:
- No MTC cases causally attributed to GLP-1 RAs in any major CVOT
- LEADER, SUSTAIN-6, SELECT: no increased thyroid malignancy signal
- Pharmacovigilance databases: case reports of MTC but no confirmed causal association
- Calcitonin levels: no consistent increase observed in clinical trials
- Population-based studies: conflicting results, no definitive signal

REGULATORY STATUS:
- Black box warning on all GLP-1 RA labels: contraindicated in patients with personal or 
  family history of MTC or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia type 2 (MEN2)
- Warning based on rodent data — precautionary principle
- FDA and EMA maintain warning despite absence of human causal evidence
- Routine calcitonin monitoring not recommended for asymptomatic patients

PAPILLARY THYROID CANCER:
- Some observational studies suggested increased papillary thyroid cancer risk
- Subsequent larger studies and meta-analyses did not confirm this association
- Current consensus: no established causal link with papillary thyroid cancer

CLINICAL GUIDANCE:
- Contraindicated: personal or family history of MTC or MEN2
- Not contraindicated: benign thyroid nodules, hypothyroidism, or other thyroid conditions
- Counsel patients about theoretical risk and signs of thyroid tumour (neck mass, dysphagia)
- Routine calcitonin or thyroid ultrasound monitoring not required in low-risk patients
- Elevated calcitonin at baseline: investigate before initiating, consider endocrinology referral

CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS:
The thyroid cancer concern is primarily based on rodent data with a species-specific mechanism 
that does not reliably translate to humans. The absolute contraindication for MTC/MEN2 history 
is appropriate. For the general population, the theoretical risk is not supported by human 
clinical trial evidence and should not prevent use when clinically indicated.

SOURCE: Bethel MA, et al. Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol. 2019;7(1):13-23. Safety analysis.

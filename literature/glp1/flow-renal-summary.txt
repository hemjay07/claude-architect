TITLE: FLOW — Semaglutide and Kidney Outcomes in Type 2 Diabetes
DRUG: Semaglutide subcutaneous 1.0mg weekly
POPULATION: 3,533 adults with T2DM, eGFR 25-75 mL/min/1.73m², UACR ≥300 mg/g
DURATION: Median 3.4 years
PRIMARY ENDPOINT: Renal composite (kidney failure, ≥50% sustained eGFR decline, renal or CV death)

KEY RESULTS:
- Primary endpoint: HR 0.76 (95% CI 0.66-0.88), p<0.001 — superiority demonstrated
- Kidney failure: HR 0.79 (95% CI 0.66-0.94) — significant
- ≥50% eGFR decline: HR 0.57 (95% CI 0.45-0.74) — significant
- CV death: HR 0.71 (95% CI 0.56-0.89) — significant
- All-cause mortality: HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.67-0.95) — significant
- eGFR slope: -2.19 vs -3.36 mL/min/1.73m²/year — slower decline with semaglutide
- UACR reduction: -32% vs placebo
- HbA1c reduction: -0.9% vs placebo
- Body weight: -3.8kg vs placebo

SAFETY SIGNALS:
- Nausea: 17% vs 9% placebo
- Serious adverse events: similar between arms
- Acute kidney injury: lower in semaglutide arm
- No new safety signals beyond established GLP-1 RA profile

CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS:
FLOW is the first dedicated renal outcomes trial for a GLP-1 RA and establishes semaglutide as a renoprotective agent in T2DM with CKD. The eGFR slope benefit suggests disease-modifying effects beyond glycemic control. Results are additive to SGLT2 inhibitor renal benefits — combination therapy is now supported in high-risk CKD patients.

SOURCE: Perkovic V, et al. N Engl J Med. 2024;390(18):1671-1683. doi:10.1056/NEJMoa2403347

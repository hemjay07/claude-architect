TITLE: STEP-1 — Semaglutide 2.4mg for Weight Management in Obesity
DRUG: Semaglutide subcutaneous 2.4mg weekly
POPULATION: 1,961 adults with BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with weight-related comorbidity, no T2DM
DURATION: 68 weeks (1.3 years)
PRIMARY ENDPOINT: Percentage change in body weight from baseline

KEY RESULTS:
- Body weight change: -14.9% vs -2.4% placebo, p<0.001
- Mean weight loss: -15.3kg vs -2.6kg placebo
- ≥5% weight loss achieved: 86% vs 32% placebo
- ≥10% weight loss achieved: 69% vs 12% placebo
- ≥15% weight loss achieved: 50% vs 5% placebo
- ≥20% weight loss achieved: 32% vs 2% placebo
- Waist circumference: -13.5cm vs -4.1cm placebo
- Systolic BP: -6.2 vs -1.4 mmHg placebo
- HbA1c: not primary endpoint (non-diabetic population)

SAFETY SIGNALS:
- Nausea: 44% vs 16% placebo — most common, mostly transient during dose escalation
- Vomiting: 25% vs 7% placebo
- Diarrhoea: 30% vs 16% placebo
- Constipation: 24% vs 11% placebo
- GI adverse events leading to discontinuation: 4.5% vs 0.8%
- Gallbladder disorders: 2.6% vs 1.2% placebo
- Heart rate increase: +1.5 bpm

CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS:
STEP-1 established semaglutide 2.4mg as the most effective pharmacological weight loss agent at the time of publication, achieving weight loss previously seen only with bariatric surgery in a subset of patients. The dose-response relationship (achieving 20%+ loss in one-third of patients) changed expectations for pharmacological obesity treatment.

SOURCE: Wilding JPH, et al. N Engl J Med. 2021;384(11):989-1002. doi:10.1056/NEJMoa2032183

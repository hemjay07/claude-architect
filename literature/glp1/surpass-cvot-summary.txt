TITLE: SURPASS-CVOT — Tirzepatide vs Semaglutide for Cardiovascular Outcomes in T2DM
DRUG: Tirzepatide 5mg, 10mg, 15mg subcutaneous weekly
COMPARATOR: Semaglutide 1.0mg subcutaneous weekly (active comparator, not placebo)
POPULATION: 13,000 adults with T2DM and established cardiovascular disease
DURATION: Median 3.4 years
PRIMARY ENDPOINT: First occurrence of 3-point MACE (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke)

KEY RESULTS:
- Primary endpoint: HR 0.85 (95% CI 0.71-1.02) vs semaglutide — non-inferiority demonstrated
- Superiority not demonstrated vs semaglutide
- HbA1c reduction: -1.24% greater reduction vs semaglutide
- Body weight: -7.8kg greater reduction vs semaglutide
- CV death: similar between arms
- Heart failure hospitalisation: similar between arms

SAFETY SIGNALS:
- GI adverse events: higher with tirzepatide vs semaglutide
- Nausea: more frequent with tirzepatide during dose escalation
- Discontinuation due to GI events: higher with tirzepatide
- No new unexpected safety signals

CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS:
SURPASS-CVOT used an active comparator design — the first major CVOT to compare two active agents rather than placebo. Non-inferiority vs semaglutide was demonstrated for CV outcomes. Tirzepatide showed superior metabolic effects (HbA1c and weight) with a less favorable GI tolerability profile. The trial does not establish CV superiority of tirzepatide over semaglutide.

SOURCE: Bhatt DL, et al. N Engl J Med. 2024;391(6):503-515. doi:10.1056/NEJMoa2406526

TITLE: Cardiovascular Pleiotropic Effects of GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
DRUG CLASS: GLP-1 receptor agonists

OVERVIEW:
The cardiovascular benefits of GLP-1 RAs extend beyond glycemic control and weight loss. 
These pleiotropic effects are observed even in non-diabetic populations (SELECT trial) 
suggesting direct cardiovascular mechanisms independent of metabolic improvement.

ATHEROSCLEROSIS:
- GLP-1 receptors expressed on macrophages, endothelial cells, and vascular smooth muscle
- Reduce macrophage foam cell formation — key step in plaque development
- Improve endothelial function — increase nitric oxide bioavailability
- Reduce vascular inflammation — lower CRP, IL-6, TNF-alpha
- Stabilise existing atherosclerotic plaques
- Effects demonstrated in human coronary artery studies

HEART FAILURE:
- Direct cardiac GLP-1 receptor activation — positive inotropic effects
- Reduce cardiac fibrosis and hypertrophy in animal models
- STEP-HFpEF: symptomatic improvement in HFpEF with obesity
- Mechanism in HFpEF likely multifactorial: weight loss + anti-inflammatory + direct cardiac effects
- Less clear benefit in HFrEF — trials ongoing

BLOOD PRESSURE:
- Consistent modest reduction in systolic BP (-2 to -5 mmHg) across trials
- Mechanism: natriuresis, weight loss, vasodilation
- Additive to other antihypertensive agents

LIPIDS:
- Modest LDL reduction (-3 to -5%)
- Triglyceride reduction (-10 to -20%)
- HDL increase (+2 to +5%)
- Effects partly mediated by weight loss, partly direct hepatic effects

HEART RATE:
- Consistent increase of +2-4 bpm across all GLP-1 RAs
- Mechanism unclear — possible sympathetic nervous system activation
- Clinical significance uncertain — no increased arrhythmia signal in trials

CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS:
The pleiotropic CV effects explain why CV benefit is observed in non-diabetic patients (SELECT) 
and why the magnitude of CV benefit exceeds what would be predicted from metabolic improvements 
alone. This supports use of GLP-1 RAs in high CV risk patients regardless of diabetic status.

SOURCE: Marso SP, McGuire DK. Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol. 2017;5(4):246-248. Review.

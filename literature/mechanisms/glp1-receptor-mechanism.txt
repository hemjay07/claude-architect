TITLE: GLP-1 Receptor Agonist Mechanism of Action
DRUG CLASS: GLP-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs)
AGENTS: Semaglutide, liraglutide, dulaglutide, exenatide, lixisenatide, tirzepatide (dual GIP/GLP-1)

MECHANISM OF ACTION:

Glycemic effects:
- GLP-1 RAs bind and activate the GLP-1 receptor, a G-protein coupled receptor
- Glucose-dependent insulin secretion: stimulate pancreatic beta cells to release insulin only when glucose is elevated — minimal hypoglycemia risk
- Suppress glucagon secretion from pancreatic alpha cells — reduces hepatic glucose output
- Slow gastric emptying — reduces postprandial glucose excursions
- Central appetite suppression via hypothalamic GLP-1 receptors — reduces food intake and body weight

Cardiovascular effects (pleiotropic):
- Direct cardiac GLP-1 receptor activation — improves myocardial function
- Anti-atherosclerotic effects — reduce vascular inflammation, improve endothelial function
- Reduce oxidative stress in vessel walls
- Modest blood pressure reduction (systolic -2 to -5 mmHg)
- Modest LDL and triglyceride reduction
- Heart rate increase (+2-4 bpm) — mechanism not fully understood, possibly sympathetic activation

Renal effects:
- Reduce intraglomerular pressure via natriuresis
- Anti-inflammatory effects in the kidney
- Reduce albuminuria independent of glycemic effects
- Mechanisms partially overlap with but are distinct from SGLT2 inhibitors

Tirzepatide (dual agonist):
- Activates both GIP and GLP-1 receptors
- GIP receptor activation contributes additional weight loss and metabolic effects
- Superior weight loss vs GLP-1 RA monotherapy (20%+ vs 15% body weight)
- CV outcome data still emerging

SOURCE: Nauck MA, D'Alessio DA. Cell Metab. 2022;35(5):706-727. Review article.

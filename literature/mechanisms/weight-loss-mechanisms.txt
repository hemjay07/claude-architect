TITLE: Mechanisms of Weight Loss with GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
DRUG CLASS: GLP-1 receptor agonists

CENTRAL MECHANISMS (primary):
- GLP-1 receptors expressed in hypothalamus, brainstem, and reward pathways
- Hypothalamic activation reduces appetite and food intake
- Brainstem (area postrema) activation contributes to nausea and satiety signaling
- Reward pathway modulation reduces food cravings and hedonic eating
- Slows gastric emptying — prolongs satiety after meals
- These central effects are dose-dependent — higher doses (2.4mg semaglutide) produce greater weight loss

PERIPHERAL MECHANISMS (secondary):
- Gastric emptying delay — reduces postprandial glucose and prolongs fullness
- Reduced glucagon secretion — decreases hepatic glucose output and may reduce fat storage
- Modest increase in energy expenditure — mechanism not fully characterised

MAGNITUDE OF WEIGHT LOSS BY AGENT:
- Liraglutide 3.0mg: approximately -8% body weight (SCALE trial)
- Semaglutide 1.0mg (diabetes dose): approximately -6% body weight
- Semaglutide 2.4mg (obesity dose): approximately -15% body weight (STEP-1)
- Tirzepatide 15mg: approximately -21% body weight (SURMOUNT-1)
- Comparison: bariatric surgery typically -25 to -35% body weight

DOSE DEPENDENCY:
- Weight loss is strongly dose-dependent for both semaglutide and tirzepatide
- Dose escalation protocols (start low, increase every 4 weeks) reduce GI adverse events
- Most weight loss occurs in first 6 months — plateau typically reached by 12-16 months
- Weight regain occurs rapidly after discontinuation — treatment is not curative

PREDICTORS OF RESPONSE:
- Early weight loss (4% at 16 weeks) predicts good long-term response
- Baseline BMI does not strongly predict magnitude of % weight loss
- Presence of T2DM attenuates weight loss vs non-diabetic patients

CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS:
The central mechanism of action explains why GLP-1 RAs produce substantially more weight loss 
than peripheral-acting agents. The dose-response relationship means obesity doses (semaglutide 2.4mg, 
tirzepatide 15mg) should be used when weight loss is the primary goal — diabetes doses 
(semaglutide 1.0mg) produce meaningful but lower weight loss.

SOURCE: Drucker DJ. Cell Metab. 2022;34(1):25-44. Review article.
